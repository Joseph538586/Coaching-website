My approach

Coaching is a space for development and reflection. It can be short-term, when the focus is on a specific challenge, or longer-term, when the goal is broader growth.

My clients are often leaders and professionals facing situations such as:

  • Navigating complex organizational changes
  • Balancing operational demands with strategic leadership
  • Clarifying their role and authority within a group
  • Managing cross-cultural dynamics in international teams
  • Dealing with uncertainty and the anxiety it generates

In our work together, you set the agenda and choose what to explore. My role is not to provide ready-made solutions but to help you uncover your own. We explore both the visible and invisible forces shaping your leadership, so you can act with clarity, confidence, and greater impact.

Grounded in the Systems–Psychodynamics tradition, my coaching offers a structured yet open space for deep reflection and strategic thinking.

About Systems Psychodynamics

The Systems Psychodynamics approach was developed in the 1950s–60s at the Tavistock Institute for Human Relations in London.

It combines insights from:

  • Psychoanalysis (to explore unconscious dynamics and defense mechanisms)
  • System theory (to analyze roles, organizations, and their environments)
  • Group relations (to experience authority, dependency, leadership, and the group as a whole)

This perspective helps organizations and individuals understand the underlying dynamics that shape behavior, decision-making, and leadership. It brings to light the human and emotional dimension that often remains hidden but strongly influences effectiveness.

Les Systèmes Psychodynamiques

L'approche Systèmes Psychodynamiques a été développée dans les années 1950–60 au Tavistock Institute for Human Relations à Londres.

Elle combine des perspectives issues de :

  • Psychanalyse (pour explorer les dynamiques inconscientes et les mécanismes de défense)
  • Théorie des systèmes (pour analyser les rôles, les organisations et leurs environnements)
  • Relations de groupe (pour expérimenter l'autorité, la dépendance, le leadership et le groupe dans son ensemble)

Cette perspective aide les organisations et les individus à comprendre les dynamiques sous-jacentes qui façonnent le comportement, la prise de décision et le leadership. Elle met en lumière la dimension humaine et émotionnelle qui reste souvent cachée mais influence fortement l'efficacité.

About me

I have been living in Asia for the past ten years, managing a small international trading office in a hybrid setup, working closely with teams in China, Brazil, and France. This experience immersed me in the complexities of cross-cultural leadership, organizational dynamics, and the pressures of international trade.

Over time, I came to see that personal fulfillment and performance are not just a matter of skills or strategy. They emerge from the alignment between a person and the larger system they operate in, and from the ability to work effectively within a group.

These reflections inspired me to become a coach. Today, I combine my professional background with the systemic–psychodynamic approach to support leaders in exploring complexity, making sense of what unfolds around them, and reconnecting with what matters most in themselves and in their work.
